# Forge Report Template

Write the report to a markdown file. Adapt headings to the premise, but keep the
skeleton below. Order matters: decision first (the reader gets the answer before the
evidence), scorecard second, then the drill-down and the evolution trail.

---

```markdown
# <Idea name> — Forge Report (<YYYY-MM>)

<One paragraph: the premise as it stands now and its load-bearing claims. Name the
method: "N independent agents, one per branch, each attacking its branch and
returning an adaptation; every non-obvious claim source-cited; M rounds of forging.">

## Bottom line

<2–4 sentences. Lead with the decision — PROCEED / EVOLVE / DROP. Say whether the
agents converged or split, and how far the idea moved from where it started.>

> **<DECISION> — <one-line reason>**

## Branch scorecard

| Branch | Verdict | Key finding & adaptation |
|---|---|---|
| <load-bearing claim 1> | **SURVIVES / ADAPT / PRUNE** | <most damaging evidence + how the idea changed, with source> |
| <load-bearing claim 2> | ... | ... |

Tally: **SURVIVES ×N · ADAPT ×N · PRUNE ×N**

## Per-branch findings

### <Branch 1> — <verdict>
<The attack: named incumbents / prior art / data / dates that falsify or weaken the
claim, each cited. Then the ADAPTATION (how the idea should change) and the SURVIVING
EDGE the finding doesn't reach. Distinguish false / true-but-irrelevant /
true-but-already-owned. If PRUNE, say plainly why it's a dead end.>

### <Branch 2> — <verdict>
...

## Evolution trail

<How the idea moved. One line per round: premise vN → vN+1 and what changed and why.
This is the record of the forging — what the research taught, branch by branch.>

- **v0** → **v1**: <what was pruned, what was reshaped, which finding drove it.>
- **v1** → **v2**: ...

## The decision

**PROCEED** — <what survives intact and why it's worth building as-is.>
**EVOLVE** — <the reshaped premise to carry forward; see Viable variants.>
**DROP** — <what to stop believing; the dead ends, so they aren't revisited.>

## Viable variants

<Reframes that survive the pruning, ranked "most alive" first. Each names what it
keeps, what it drops, why it survives the finding that pruned the original, and one
concrete next step to validate it. Include wedges surfaced from incumbent holes
(reviews/complaints). Mark each analyst-confirmed vs inference-only.>

1. **<Variant name>** — <keeps / drops / why it survives / first validation step.>

## What changed / new intel

<Facts the premise predates and should absorb — new competitors, new prior art,
regulatory dates, demand signals — anything that moved since it was written.>

## Caveats

<Where figures are approximate (aggregator/press-sourced), which branches were
inference vs verified, what could not be confirmed and why. The report's value
depends on the reader trusting its limits.>
```

---

## Verdict vocabulary

Per-branch:
- **SURVIVES** — holds up against the strongest attack the agent could mount.
- **ADAPT** — the broad claim doesn't hold, but a reshaped version does; the finding
  tells the idea how to change.
- **PRUNE** — falsified, or true-but-irrelevant, or owned by someone the idea-holder
  can't out-execute. A dead end; record it and don't revisit.

Overall decision:
- **PROCEED** — the load-bearing branches survive; build the premise roughly as stated.
- **EVOLVE** — the premise-as-stated is reshaped by the adaptations into a stronger
  version worth carrying into another round or into execution. The common, healthy
  outcome for a serious idea.
- **DROP** — nothing load-bearing survives and no variant is worth the hours.
